Experience Replay for Continual Learning

David Rolnick, Arun Ahuja, Jonathan Schwarz, Timothy P. Lillicrap, Greg Wayne

NeurIPS 2019

Paper Adress

Introduction

这篇文章是在研究强化学习与持续学习如何进行结合，即如何解决强化学习中的灾难性遗忘（catastrophic forgetting）问题。

In fact, boundaries between tasks will often be unknown – or tasks will deform continuously and not have definite boundaries at all [12]. Such a paradigm for training eliminates the possibility of simultaneously acting upon and learning from several tasks, and leads to the possibility of catastrophic forgetting.

之前解决强化学习中的灾难性遗忘问题的方法大多聚焦于 synaptic consolidation approaches，“经验重放（experience replay）”的方法一直被忽略了。

Main Idea

We here demonstrate that replay can be a powerful tool in continual learning. We propose a simple technique, Continual Learning with Experience And Replay (CLEAR), mixing on-policy learning from novel experiences (for plasticity) and off-policy learning from replay experiences (for stability).

CLEAR 比较亮眼的一个特点：

No task information. CLEAR does not rely upon the identity of tasks or the boundaries between them, by contrast with prior art. This means that CLEAR can be applied to a much broader range of situations, where tasks are unknown or are not discrete.

In settings where data is streaming without clear task boundaries, Rolnick et al. [45] suggest reservoir sampling to limit the number of stored samples to a fixed budget assuming an overall i.i.d. distributed data stream.
